Labels and Placeholders

  • Top positioning improves the association between the label and field
  • However, it tends to give the impression of a longer form
  • Useful for very familiar, short forms like sign-in forms

Labels and Placeholders

  • Right positioning reduces the vertical space of the web form, and improves the association between the label and input
  • However, right-positioned labels can reduce the readability and scanability of web forms

Labels and Placeholders

  • When the data being collected is unfamiliar or complex, labels should be placed to the left of the field, and should be left-aligned
  • This helps visitors scan down the list of labels so that they can see exactly what they will be providing

Labels and Placeholders

  • Placeholders are great to give the user a hint of the data they need to provide
  • You can even get away with no labels, just placeholders, for very familiar forms
  • However, once the user enters data, the hint cannot be referred back to for review

Autosuggest

  • Saves the user from typing complete data, minimizing errors
  • Useful for data types that belong to a "set"
  • e.g. tags, categories, countries, dropdowns

Other Tips

  • Preserve all customer input despite errors in the form
  • Validate form fields inline
  • Disable the paste-function in the ‘E-mail confirmation’ field
